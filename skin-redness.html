Skin Concern · Redness

Redness & Flushing.

Distinguish temporary flushing from chronic redness, and the ingredients that genuinely calm reactive skin.

How to know if this is you

LUMEVA
01
Redness across cheeks and nose.

A butterfly-shaped pattern of redness across the central face is a classic presentation, often linked to rosacea or chronic vascular reactivity.

LUMEVA
02
Flushing triggered by heat or alcohol.

Rapid flushing after hot drinks, alcohol, spicy food, or exercise indicates vascular hyperreactivity — blood vessels dilating more readily than average.

LUMEVA
03
Visible small blood vessels.

Telangiectasia (visible thread veins, usually on cheeks and nose) develop from repeated vessel dilation over time and are a hallmark of advanced rosacea.

LUMEVA
04
Redness that worsens with skincare products.

If active ingredients consistently cause flushing rather than just tingling, your skin's vascular response is more reactive than typical sensitivity.

LUMEVA
05
Bumps that resemble acne but don't respond to acne treatment.

Papulopustular rosacea mimics acne but does not improve (and often worsens) with typical acne treatments like benzoyl peroxide.

LUMEVA
06
Family history of rosacea or chronic flushing.

Rosacea has a strong genetic component — a parent with persistent facial redness significantly increases your likelihood of the same pattern.

Root causes

Understanding the cause helps you target the right solution rather than guessing.

🩸

Vascular hyperreactivity

Blood vessels in reactive skin dilate more readily and stay dilated longer than average, causing visible, persistent redness.

🌶️

Common triggers

Heat, alcohol, spicy food, sun exposure, and stress are the most frequently reported triggers for flare-ups in redness-prone skin.

🦠

Demodex mites

An overgrowth of normally-present skin mites is linked to rosacea-type redness and is treated with specific antiparasitic approaches.

🧬

Genetics

Rosacea and chronic redness run strongly in families, particularly in fair-skinned populations of Northern European descent, though it occurs in all skin tones.

☀️

Cumulative UV damage

Sun exposure damages vessel walls over years, contributing to the visible thread veins seen in chronic redness.

🧴

Irritant skincare ingredients

Alcohol-based toners, harsh acids, and physical scrubs provoke vascular flushing and can worsen baseline redness over time.

Dermatologist note

Persistent facial redness lasting more than a few months, especially with visible blood vessels or acne-like bumps that don't respond to standard acne treatment, is frequently rosacea — a manageable but chronic condition that benefits significantly from proper diagnosis. Over-the-counter calming skincare helps manage symptoms, but prescription options (topical metronidazole, azelaic acid, oral antibiotics, or laser treatment for visible vessels) are far more effective for moderate-to-severe cases. Please see a dermatologist if redness is persistent and affecting your confidence — this is one of the most treatable conditions in dermatology.
